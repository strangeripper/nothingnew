Arabian Coffee (قهوة عربية)

Arabian coffee — often called qahwa — is lightly roasted, spiced (cardamom is common), and traditionally served from a dallah into small cups called finjān.

Quick facts

  • Roast: light to blonde; beans may appear yellowish after brewing.
  • Common spices: cardamom, saffron, cloves.
  • Etiquette: Serve from the right; guests usually take 1–3 cups.

Simple recipe (home version)

  1. Bring water to a gentle boil.
  2. Add freshly ground light-roast coffee; simmer a few minutes.
  3. Stir in cracked cardamom (and a pinch of saffron, optional).
  4. Let grounds settle; pour into a thermos or dallah; serve hot.
